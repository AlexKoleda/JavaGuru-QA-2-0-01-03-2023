Daugavpils is a state city in Latvia. Its total area is 72.48 km2 and the population in 2020 was 82,046.[3] It is the second largest city in Latvia in terms of population after Riga. Daugavpils is located in the southeast of the country by the Daugava River. The city is located on the southeastern edge of the East Latvian lowland. Most of the city is located in the bed of the ancient Daugava, which is a saura in its territory. The city is located on both banks of the Daugava, both in Latgale and Selia. The length of the river in the city territory is approximately 16 km. Daugavpils is the largest city of the Latgale planning region, the center of industry and education, as well as an important center of culture, sports and recreation. The city is also an important center of socio-economic development in the Baltics.
