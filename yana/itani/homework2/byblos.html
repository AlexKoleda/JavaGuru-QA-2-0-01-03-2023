Byblos

Byblos is one of the 20 oldest cities in the world. It has been continuously inhabited ever since it was established in 5,000 B.C.
According to a legend, the city was built by the god Cronus as the first city in Phoenicia. In Greek mythology, Cronus is the titan of the harvest.
Byblos is associated with the history of the diffusion of the Phoenician alphabet.
The first inscription in the Phoenician alphabet was found on the sarcophagus of Ahiram who was the king of Byblos!
Byblos was located on the trade route where merchants exported papyrus from Egypt to Greece.
Byblos was officially recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1984.
