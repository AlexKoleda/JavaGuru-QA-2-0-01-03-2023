First mention of Beirut is from the 15th century BC
Beirut derives its name from the Caananite-Phoenician word "bee-root" meaning "wells"
The city first came to prominence when it became a Roman colony in 14 BC
The Roman city was basically destroyed in 551 AD due to a series of earthquakes and a resulting tidal wave
Beirut was rebuilt by Muslim conquerors following their occupation of the city in 635 AD
Under Ottoman rule, Beirut became the capital of a large coastal area between Palestine and Syria
The American University of Beirut is the oldest university in Lebanon, founded in 1866
By 1900, Beirut was viewed as the leader of Arabic journalism
The pupulation of the city is equally populated by Christians and Muslims
Beirut was occupied by allied forces after World War I and was part of the French Mandate from 1920 to 1943 until Lebanon was granted independence
